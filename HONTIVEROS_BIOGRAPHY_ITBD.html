Experiences

Childhood Years


My childhood years was fun.1 It was full of playing outside and inside our house.2 It was full of innocence and not a single bit of worry about my surroundings.3 I also got to play In school.4 Our activities were all about how to make studying fun.5 After school, there are vendors outside the school.6 some of them sell toys while some of them sell snacks.7 I keep my 20 pesos lunch money until school is done so I can buy delicious snacks outside.8 Sometimes, I use it to buy small 5 pesos toys.9 I can not help myself, I was just a child full of innocence.10 I also watch morning and afternoon cartoons.11 Whenever I go outside to play with my friends, one of them will scream “DORAEMON NA!” then we will all go home to watch cartoons.12 I got most of the cartoons theme song memorized,I would sing them non-stop.13 I spent most of my childhood years outside, Playing hopscotch,hide-n-seek, and sometime we play langit-lupa.14 Sometimes, I get wounded from playing and go home crying.15 One time I pretended to be wonder woman and jumped from the stairs.16 My chin got injured which left a mark on me, but it was worth it.17 I wish I could go back to the good old times.18 I wish I can still spend my time not worrying about the world.19 Being a child is really fun when you are full of innocence and curiosity.20

Teenage Years


   In my teenage years, I was a slacker.1 I was not taking my life seriously.2 I have not decided about my career yet or what I want to do with my life.3 After school I would go home immediately to play games.4 Sometime, I would skip some classes just so I can play games.5 I had too much confidence in myself for the reason that I would get high marks in quizzes and tests without studying.6 i did not participate in events or activities.7 Instead, I would go look for a quiet place to hang out by myself and enjoy my time.8 My senior years came, and I am still a slacker.9 I tried to give a little bit of effort on my assignments.10 But I still ended up spending more time playing games or hanging out with my classmates.11 Sometimes, we go to mall or go to cinema to watch movies.12 It was all fun and games, until I saw my classmates worrying about their future.13 Everyone are thinking about their future and their career path.14 Everyone was working hard for their future while I spend my time playing games.15 Then, I started thinking to myself what will happen to me.16 If I keep on slacking what will happen to my future and my life.17 So, I tried to change myself for the better.18 I tried spending less time on playing games and more on studying for my career choice.19 I know it may sound funny but, I want to work on my future for the reason that if I succeed in my life, I will be able to buy more games and consoles.20

College Years


    When I started college, I had no idea what I was getting myself into.1 I was only 17 years old at that time.2 which made me feel insignificant because everyone around me appeared to be grown enough to deal with life's difficulties.3 I was having trouble adjusting to campus because there are so many events and activities to participate in until the epidemic struck.4 Our school incorporated online programs.5 which simply added to the difficulty of studying.6 I couldn't hear what the professor was saying, and my surroundings aren't conducive to learning.7 So I had to take a break for a year.8 thinking that we would be able to do face-to-face schooling the next year.9 We were not allowed to go out while I was on leave from school.10 As a result, I attempted to learn new hobbies.11 Reading novels or strumming the guitar, for example.12 I also attempted to learn game programming.13 Sometime, I worry about my future.14 And whenever that happens I remind myself of the quote.15

"If you want to be happy, do not dwell in the past, do no worry about the future, focus on living fully in the present."16
- Roy T. Bennett

It lifted my spirits up and made me hopeful about my life.17 And now I am back to school. 18 I am ready to study and learn for my future career.19 Life may be hard, but I will learn to adapt and grow.20
