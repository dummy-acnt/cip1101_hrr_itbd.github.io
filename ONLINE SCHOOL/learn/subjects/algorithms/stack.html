Stacks

Stacks can be easily imagined as a pile of objects stacked vertically. when extracting these objects, they are extracted in order from top to down. When adding data to a stack, the data is put into the lowest available section. When extracting data from a stack, the most recently added data is removed first.

The method of extracting the most recently added data first is called "LIFO" or "Last In First Out"


Push - refers to the act of adding data to a stack.

Pop - refers to the act of extracting data from a stack.
